When science fiction book series The Expanse first introduces us to Avasarala, a Deputy Undersecretary of the United Nations, she is the lone woman at a roundtable of men who are debating the details of a brewing war. She plucks pistachos from her purse, seemingly more interested in eating than in the conversation at hand. A particular emphasis is given to her appearance; she wears a traditional sari in a bright orange that contrasts to the somber shades of men's formalwear. Fans of sci-fi and fantasy will recognize the trope in play: a token woman sits by as little more than a beautiful accessory while the men handle all the tough decisions. Nothing interesting to see here, right?
